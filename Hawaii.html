Hawaii


Known for its winter season, Hawaii has held its name as the capital of surfing unchallenged. Professionals travel from every corner of the globe for a Pipeline barrel as spectators cheer on the white sand beaches. Careful with these locals they do not take it easy on the haoles.






Best Season

Months of November-February (winter months). Average Swell Height (Measured from the Back): 5-7 ft. Average Swell Period: 10-12 Seconds. Prevalent Winds: Offshore (South East/ SSE /ESE). Prevalent Swell Direction: West North West / North West. Be aware most surf spots in Hawaii are Reef breaks. Be prepared for cuts, avoid infection. Locals are aggressive and competitive. Surfing in Hawaii can be stressful, wait your turn in the lineup. Locals are known for taking fights into the beach. If you plan on starting fights, it is recommended to take a couple months of Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu training lessons. Visit Gracies Jiu-Jitsu Website to sign up for a lesson before your trip.



Equipment

Average Water Temp: 25˚ C / 77˚ F. Invest in a comfortable spring-suit or a neoprene long-sleeve. If you are thick skinned boardshorts will do fine. Average Air Temp: 23˚-32˚ C / 75˚-90˚ F. Pack light, be prepared for blistering hot days. Invest in strong sunscreen (100 SPF+). Recommend having a strong 6-8ft leash and a 10-12ft backup leash.



Expected Travel Cost (JFK)

Keep your eyes open for deals. It is possible to get a roundtrip flight for under $500 in the months of October-November or February-April. Vacation months will cost more, but it is possible to find deals for under $1000. Know your airlines policy on bringing boards, check out Surfline's list of the most accomodating Airlines.
