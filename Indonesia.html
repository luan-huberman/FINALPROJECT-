Indonesia


Known for its lefts, Indonesia offers consistent swell from April until October. Some of the best surfers in the world gather here to surf some of the best waves our world has to offer. Here's what you need to know before you go.






Best Season

Months of April through October (winter months/ "dry season"). Average Swell Height (Measured from the Back): 4-6 ft. Average Swell Period: 10-12 Seconds. Prevalent Winds: Offshore (West/ WNW). Prevalent Swell Direction: South. Be aware most surf spots in Indonesia are Reef breaks. Be prepared for cuts, avoid infection. Locals are generally friendly, if an altercation occurs you will be treated as an outsider and subjected to local laws. There is not much law enforcement, it is best to respect others and earn your respect.



Equipment

Average Water Temp: 28˚-31˚ C / 82˚- 88˚ F. Boardshorts are a must have. Invest in boardshorts that offer the best overall comfort for long sessions. If you get cold easily it is suggested to pack a 1-2 mm neoprene vest or longsleeve shirt. Average Air Temp: 23˚-32˚ C / 75˚-90˚ F. Pack light, be prepared for blistering hot days. Invest in strong sunscreen (100 SPF+). Recommend having a strong 6-8ft leash and a 10-12ft backup leash.



Expected Travel Cost (JFK)

Keep your eyes open for deals. It is possible to get a roundtrip flight for under $1000 in the months of March-May or August-October. Months of June-July will cost more, but it is possible to find deals for under $1600. Be aware of long layovers. Know your airlines policy on bringing boards, check out Surfline's list of the most accomodating Airlines.
